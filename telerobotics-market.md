<p><p>Telerobotics market types refer to the different ways in which robots can be controlled remotely. Wireless network connections involve using Wi-Fi or cellular networks to transmit commands to the robot, allowing for greater flexibility and mobility. Tethered connections, on the other hand, require a physical connection between the control device and the robot, typically through cables. While wireless connections offer more freedom of movement, tethered connections can provide more stability and reliability in certain applications. Both types have their own advantages and limitations in the telerobotics market.</p></p>
