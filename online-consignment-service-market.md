<p><p>Outward consignment market refers to a scenario where a seller sends goods to a consignment store to be sold on their behalf. The consignment store acts as an agent, selling the goods and taking a commission. Inward consignment market, on the other hand, refers to a situation where a buyer purchases goods from a consignment store and the store acts as an agent for the seller. In both cases, the consignment store facilitates the transaction between the buyer and seller, taking a cut of the sale price as a commission.</p></p>
