<p><ul><li>Grid-connected Microgrid</li><li>Off-grid Microgrid</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Grid-connected Microgrid refers to a system that is connected to the main utility grid, allowing for two-way flow of electricity. This type of microgrid provides flexibility in managing energy demand and supply, while also enhancing grid reliability and resilience. On the other hand, Off-grid Microgrid operates independently of the main utility grid, often using renewable energy sources like solar and wind. This solution is suitable for remote or isolated areas where grid connection is not feasible, providing self-sufficiency and energy security.</p></p>
