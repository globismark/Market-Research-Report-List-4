<p><strong>Market Segmentation</strong></p>
<p><strong>The Surgical 3D Printing Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Stereolithography, SLA (Liquid Based)</li><li>Fused Deposition Modeling, FDM (Solid Based)</li><li>Selective Laser Sintering, SLS ( Powder Based)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Stereolithography (SLA) involves using a liquid-based material that is cured by a laser to create precise surgical models. Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM) utilizes a solid-based material that is heated and extruded through a nozzle to build surgical structures layer by layer. Selective Laser Sintering (SLS) uses a powder-based material that is fused together by a laser to produce detailed surgical components. Each type of 3D printing technology offers unique advantages for creating custom surgical instruments and implants.</p></p>
