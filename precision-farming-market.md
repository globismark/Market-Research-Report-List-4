Guidance System: This technology uses GPS to accurately guide farming equipment, reducing overlap and enhancing efficiency.</p><p>2. Remote Sensing: Utilizing drones and satellites, remote sensing technology provides detailed data on crop health, soil conditions, and weather patterns to optimize farming practices.</p><p>3. Variable-Rate Technology: This technology allows farmers to apply inputs such as seeds, fertilizers, and pesticides at varying rates based on specific conditions in different parts of the field, leading to increased productivity and cost savings.</p></p>
