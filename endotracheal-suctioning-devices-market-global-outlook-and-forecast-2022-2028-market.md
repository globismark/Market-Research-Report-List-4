<p><p>Endotracheal suctioning devices are used to remove secretions from the airway in patients with artificial airways. The market is segmented into closed system and open system devices. Closed system devices have a prepackaged suction catheter that is enclosed in a sealed chamber to minimize the risk of contamination, while open system devices require the manual assembly of the suction catheter. Both types of devices are essential for maintaining airway patency and preventing complications in patients requiring mechanical ventilation.</p></p>
